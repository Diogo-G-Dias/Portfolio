Overview

My Amusement Park is a time management and tycoon mobile game for iOS and Android where players design, build, and operate their own amusement park empire. This published product challenges players to strategically place attractions, manage resources, and keep visitors happy while expanding their park into the ultimate entertainment destination.

Key Features

Park Building & Customization

Design and build your dream amusement park with dozens of unique rides, attractions, and amenities. Customize layouts, themes, and decorations to create a park that reflects your vision.

Visitor Management

Manage visitor needs and expectations by providing food, restrooms, entertainment, and efficient pathways. Happy visitors spend more money and recommend your park to others.

Economic Simulation

Balance income from ticket sales and in-park purchases against maintenance costs, staff salaries, and expansion expenses. Strategic pricing and investment decisions are key to success.

Staff Management

Hire and train staff members including ride operators, janitors, entertainers, and security. Assign them to optimal locations to maximize park efficiency and visitor satisfaction.

Design Challenge

Creating an intuitive building system that worked well on small mobile screens presented significant UX challenges. Early playtests showed that players struggled with precise placement of attractions and often became frustrated when trying to build their dream parks on smaller devices.

What I Changed

  • Developed a smart grid-based placement system with visual guides
  • Implemented a zoom and pan control scheme optimized for touch
  • Created a multi-step undo/redo system to reduce frustration
  • Added visual and haptic feedback for successful and invalid placements
  • Designed a contextual tutorial that guides players through their first builds

Outcome

The redesigned building system resulted in a 27% increase in session length and a 35% reduction in tutorial dropout rates. Playtest feedback specifically praised the intuitive controls. The changes also led to more creative park designs as players felt confident experimenting with complex layouts.

What I Learned

Mobile UX design requires special consideration for touch input precision and screen size limitations
