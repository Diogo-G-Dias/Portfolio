Overview

Limited Survive is a hypercasual survival game for web browsers where players fend off endless waves of undead enemies using simple one-touch controls. This published HTML5 game features a roguelike upgrade system where players select from random power-ups to create unique builds each run, all while maintaining the distinctive minimalist art style of the "Limited" series.

The game combines strategic decision-making with fast-paced action, requiring players to carefully manage resources, select powerful upgrades, and navigate dangerous environments. With its accessible controls (WASD, arrow keys, or mouse drag for movement) and intuitive upgrade system, Limited Survive delivers an intense gaming experience that's both challenging and rewarding.

Key Features

Versatile Controls

Play your way with multiple control options - WASD keys, arrow keys, or click and drag with the mouse for intuitive movement across dangerous terrain.

Strategic Upgrades

Select from various powerful upgrades throughout your run, creating unique build combinations that dramatically change your survival strategy.

Design Challenge

Ensuring all upgrade paths were viable without creating overpowered combinations proved to be a significant design challenge. Early playtests revealed that players would quickly discover and exploit certain upgrade combinations, leading to repetitive gameplay and diminished replayability.

What I Changed

  • Implemented a dynamic upgrade offering system that analyzes the player's current build
  • Created a balanced upgrade tree with complementary but not game-breaking synergies
  • Introduced conditional upgrades that become more or less powerful based on other selected abilities
  • Established a tier system for upgrades to ensure power progression feels meaningful

Outcome

The redesigned upgrade system increased average play session length by 42% and improved retention metrics. Analytics showed players experimenting with a much wider variety of builds, with no single strategy dominating the meta. Player feedback praised the "endless possibilities" and "interesting choices" in each run.

What I Learned

Player perception of choice is just as important as mathematical balance in creating engaging gameplay

Roguelike systems benefit from both randomness and carefully designed constraints
